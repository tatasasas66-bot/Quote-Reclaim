# 5-minute founder-led demo / Autopsy script

The demo IS the Autopsy. There are two versions of this script:

1. **Async Autopsy** — a 5-minute Loom delivered to anyone who
   sent in their 10 estimates. No live conversation.
2. **Live demo** — a 15-minute Calendly call. Goes deeper, asks the
   close question.

Both scripts use the **contractor's actual numbers**, not example
data. If they didn't send 10 estimates, do not run the live demo —
ask them to send 10 first.

The 5-minute Autopsy converts higher because it's done with their
numbers and shareable. The live demo converts when there's hesitation
on $49.

---

## What you do NOT say in either demo

- **"We'll recover the money for you."** (Not true. The contractor
  recovers the money by sending the message.)
- **"Guaranteed."** (No guarantees of any kind.)
- **"This is like a CRM but better."** (We are not a CRM. Don't
  invite the comparison.)
- **"You'll save X hours a week."** (We have no data on hours saved.)
- **"AI."** (Don't claim AI does anything other than generate the
  follow-up text. Even then, prefer "writes the follow-up.")
- **"It'll pay for itself."** (Let the Paid-For-Itself Meter say it.
  It's in the product, on their numbers, with math shown.)
- **"Subscribe now."** (Billing is disabled. Saying "subscribe" is
  dishonest.)
- **"How are you handling follow-up today?"** — the question is
  fine, but don't lead with it. Show the value first; ask the
  question to anchor the close.

## What you DO say

- **Their actual numbers, out loud.** "Khaled Hassan at $22,000. Tom
  at $12,000. Jane at $8,500."
- **"Sitting silent."** Not "lost," not "owed," not "stuck."
- **"Worth chasing."** Not "you should chase," not "you have to."
- **"One job back covers [N] months."** Read the Paid-For-Itself
  Meter out loud. Let the math speak.
- **"First 3 are free, no card."** Every time price comes up.
- **"Billing's being updated."** Honest about the current state.
- **"Want me to switch the free queue on for you today?"** The close.

## Async Autopsy — 5 minutes, recorded in Loom

The contractor sent 10 estimates. You're recording at your desk.
Single take. Loom thumbnail uses your face.

### (0:00 – 0:15) Opening — name them, frame it

> Hey [FIRST], this is [YOU] from Quote Reclaim. This is your
> Silent Estimate Autopsy. I've got your 10 estimates open, and I'm
> going to walk through what I'm seeing in about 5 minutes.

### (0:15 – 0:45) Paste them in — show the audit transition

Open `quotereclaim.com/onboarding/reveal`. Pick the trade (roofing).
Paste their 10 lines in the textarea. Click **Scan my quotes**.
Click **Reveal what's sitting quiet**.

> Picking the trade, pasting your 10. Hitting reveal.

Let the 3-second audit transition play. Don't talk over it.

### (0:45 – 1:30) The reveal — read the total

The Sitting Quiet number renders.

> So [first] — that's **$[total]** sitting silent across 10
> estimates. The oldest is [N] days quiet.

Pause. Let the silence work.

> Quote Reclaim ranks the ones still worth chasing first.

### (1:30 – 2:30) The Top 3 — name them

Point at the Top 3 Moves list.

> Top three are: [Name 1] at $[X], [Name 2] at $[Y], [Name 3] at $[Z].
> [Name 1] is the biggest dollar — that's the one I'd work first.

Click into the first quote. Show the 5-message recovery plan.

### (2:30 – 3:30) The recovery plan — read Day 1 and Day 3 out loud

> Day 1 — what Quote Reclaim would send [Name 1]:
>
> *"Hey [client first] — wanted to follow up on the roofing
> estimate. Anything land or fall off on your end?"*
>
> Day 3 if no reply:
>
> *"[Client first], still keeping you on the active list. Want
> me to hold the slot, or set it aside?"*

(Read the actual messages off the screen. Don't paraphrase.)

> Each one has a "Why this works" line and a Copy button. So if
> email's off, you copy and text.

### (3:30 – 4:15) Dashboard — show the Paid-For-Itself Meter

Sign in (or show a screenshot). Scroll to the dashboard aside.

> On the dashboard, this panel runs the math:
>
> *"One job back covers [N] months. Your biggest quiet quote —
> [Name 1], $[X] — covers [N] months of Quote Reclaim on its own."*
>
> That's the contractor's own numbers, not marketing copy.

### (4:15 – 4:50) Price and what's next

> Pricing is $49/month. First 3 quotes are free, no card needed.
> Billing's being updated right now, so until then I activate the
> free queue for people by hand. The folks who used the Autopsy
> get founding pricing locked in when billing relaunches.

### (4:50 – 5:00) The close

> If you want me to switch your free queue on today, reply to
> this email with the word **activate**. If this isn't for you,
> reply **kill it** and you'll never hear from me again.
>
> Either way, thanks for sending those over.

End the recording.

**Send the video. In the email body, include:**

- A bullet list of the Top 3 with dollar amounts (the value
  anchors them every time they look at it).
- The link to the live dashboard.
- The "reply activate / kill it" line.

## Live demo — 15 minutes on Zoom

If the Autopsy email gets a "can we hop on a quick call" reply, this
is the live version. 15 minutes hard cap.

### (0:00 – 0:30) Opening question

Do NOT introduce the product first. Ask:

> Hey [FIRST]. Real quick before I show anything — what's the
> last estimate you remember writing up where you never heard
> back?

Listen. They will name a real person and a real number. Write the
amount down on screen if you can.

### (0:30 – 1:30) Two more questions to deepen the pain

> What was the dollar amount on it? — Got it, $[X].
>
> And what did you end up doing about it?

This is the pain question. The answer is almost always *"nothing"*
or *"I sent one more email, then stopped."* That answer is the
product's reason to exist.

### (1:30 – 4:30) Run the reveal on their real 10

Share your screen. Open `quotereclaim.com/onboarding/reveal`. Paste
their 10. Click reveal.

Same script as the async Autopsy — but pause after the reveal:

> So [FIRST] — what number did you have in your head before you
> sent these in?

(They almost always say something lower than what shows up.)

> The product's job is just to put that number where you can see
> it every day.

### (4:30 – 8:00) Walk the Top 3 + the recovery plan

Same as async. Read Day 1, Day 3, Day 7 out loud from the actual
plan rendered for *their* top quote. Pause and ask:

> Would you send something like that?

Most contractors say *"yeah, that's basically what I'd write."*
That's the validation moment.

### (8:00 – 10:00) Dashboard + Paid-For-Itself Meter

Show the dashboard. Let the meter render off their biggest quote.

> Quote Reclaim is $49/month. Your biggest quote, [Name], $[X], is
> [N] months of the product covered if it comes back. The math is
> on the screen, you can pull up the calculator.

Don't say "isn't that cheap?" — let them say it.

### (10:00 – 12:00) Q&A

Stop showing. Camera on. Ask:

> What's the part that doesn't make sense to you?

Listen for the real objection. Use the objection scripts in
`OUTREACH_SCRIPTS.md`. Don't argue. If they say "I have a CRM," use
the CRM reply. If they say "I don't pay for software," use that
reply.

### (12:00 – 14:00) The close — three explicit options

> Three options:
>
> 1. I activate the free queue for you today. You import your top
>    3, see how it feels for a week. No card.
> 2. You take what you saw and don't sign up. I won't email again.
> 3. You want the founding rate locked in for when billing
>    relaunches — I'll put a note on your account.
>
> Which one?

Most contractors pick option 1 or 3. If they pick 2, ask:

> What would have to be true for option 1 to make sense?

That answer is gold for the next demo.

### (14:00 – 15:00) Wrap

If they said yes to option 1:
> Cool — I'll send you the sign-up link in a sec, and once you're in
> I'll personally walk through the import with you. Should take 10
> minutes max.

If they said yes to option 3:
> Done — I'll email you the moment billing relaunches, with the
> founding rate locked in. Nothing else from me in the meantime
> unless you want it.

If they said no:
> All good. Last thing — what would have to change for this to be
> a yes in 6 months? — Got it. Thanks for the time, [FIRST].

Hang up.

## After-demo follow-up (within 2 hours)

Send this email immediately after the demo, regardless of outcome:

> [FIRST],
>
> Thanks for the time. Quick recap so you have it:
>
> - **Biggest silent quote we saw:** [Name 1] at $[X]
> - **Total sitting quiet across the 10 you sent:** $[total]
> - **Months of Quote Reclaim covered by one job back:** [N]
> - **Status today:** free queue [activated / available to
>   activate / not activated per your call]
> - **Billing:** updating; founding rate will be $49/month
>
> If anything changes, you have my email. If you want the free
> queue activated now and you weren't sure on the call, just reply
> **activate** and I'll switch it on this afternoon.
>
> — [YOU]

This email is the asset. It's what the contractor forwards to their
spouse, their bookkeeper, or their estimator. It's why every demo
has to use their actual numbers.

## Common mistakes to avoid in the demo

1. **Leading with the product.** Always ask the pain question
   first. Their answer makes the demo feel personal.
2. **Saying "AI."** The product does not do AI. It generates
   follow-up text from templates. Say "writes the follow-up," not
   "AI writes it."
3. **Going past 15 minutes.** Contractor time is the most expensive
   thing in this whole plan. Respect the hard cap.
4. **Saying "subscribe today."** Billing is disabled. The honest
   close is "activate the free queue" or "lock in founding rate."
5. **Dismissing "I have a CRM."** A CRM is a contact list. Quote
   Reclaim is a silent-estimate ranker. They are different problems.
   Don't argue the point — show the Top 3 list and let it explain
   itself.
6. **Hiding the price.** $49/month, said out loud, with the math
   anchor. Never make the contractor ask.
7. **Forgetting the follow-up email.** Send it within 2 hours.
   Every demo without an email follow-up is a demo wasted.

## 1. Executive strategy

**One sentence.** We will not buy attention. We will run a free, personal
**Silent Estimate Autopsy** for one specific contractor niche, in one
specific metro, using the contractor's own 10 last estimates — and
convert the contractors who feel the pain through founder-led follow-up,
not a checkout button we don't have.

Why this works under the constraint: the product itself (Silent Money
Reveal + 5-message recovery plan + Paid-For-Itself Meter) already does
~80% of the persuasion. The Autopsy is the wrapper that gets it in
front of a real contractor's actual numbers.

## 3. Best first niche

**Independent roofing contractors, 1–15 employees, non-storm-chaser, in
ONE mid-sized US metro you can drive to.**

Why this wins as the first beachhead, not "all contractors":

1. **Ticket size weaponizes the math.** A $12,000 roof ÷ $49 = 244
   months of Quote Reclaim. The Paid-For-Itself Meter screenshots
   sell themselves.
2. **Bidding behavior is built into the trade.** Homeowners get 2–3
   roofing bids. By definition most of the bidder's quotes go quiet.
3. **Independent roofers are SaaS-light.** Storm chasers and
   franchises buy enterprise CRMs; the 5–15-person owner-operator does
   follow-up on a notepad. We are the only tool aimed at exactly that
   gap.
4. **Easy to find at zero cost.** Google Maps → "roofing contractor
   [metro]" → click each pin → grab name + phone + email + Facebook
   page. 50 prospects in 90 minutes.
5. **One language: dollars.** Roofers think in jobs and dollars. The
   product's vocabulary (silent / quiet / sitting / recover) maps 1:1.

Disqualifiers we deliberately skip on the first pass:

- Storm chasers (different sales cycle, transactional).
- Franchises (already on a corporate CRM).
- 50+ employee shops (have an estimator and a system).
- Commercial roofers (procurement, not silence).

**Pick the metro you can drive to.** A founder who can show up in person
for the first 1–3 demos has a meaningfully higher close rate than one
running cold over Zoom only. Houston, Tampa, Phoenix, Charlotte, Kansas
City, Columbus, Nashville, San Antonio, Atlanta suburbs — any of these
have hundreds of independent roofers.

## 5. Offer design

**The winning offer**

> **The Silent Estimate Autopsy.** Send me your last 10 estimates —
> name and amount only, no addresses, no phone numbers — and I'll run
> them through Quote Reclaim and send you back a 5-minute video. You
> see what's recoverable, the recovery plan for the top 3, and the
> total in dollars sitting silent. No cost. No card. Your data, your
> queue, yours to use.

Why this wins over the alternatives:

| Offer | Why rejected |
|---|---|
| A. Free Silent Quote Audit (no founder commitment) | Too soft — no specific ask, no reason to open the email. |
| B. "Recover-map 3 old estimates" | Too small for the math to be impressive. |
| **C. "Send me 10 old estimates"** | **Concrete number, concrete deliverable, concrete proof** ← winner |
| D. "Founding Contractor" | Premature — needs live billing. |
| E. "First 3 free" | Already the product's onboarding. Not a campaign. |

The Autopsy is **asymmetric**: 5 minutes of founder time per prospect,
100× perceived value because it's personalized and uses their actual
numbers. The Paid-For-Itself Meter renders **automatically** off their
real quote — the product makes the case, the founder just clicks
record.

**What you tell them when billing is disabled:**

> Billing's being updated right now — when it goes live, you'll get
> founding pricing ($49/month). Until then, your queue is on the free
> plan and the first 3 quotes run end-to-end. Anything beyond that, I
> activate by hand for the people who used the Autopsy.

Honest, no fake checkout, captures intent.

## 8. The unfair creative angle — "Silent Estimate Autopsy"

**The mechanic.** A 5-minute personalized video, recorded by the
founder, for a real contractor, using their actual 10 last estimates.

**Why it's unfair:**

1. **It is the product, used live, on their numbers.** Not a generic
   demo. The Paid-For-Itself Meter shows their actual biggest quote;
   the Top 3 Moves list uses their actual clients.
2. **It costs the founder 10 minutes per prospect.** Asymmetric: a
   contractor would pay a consultant $250 for the same exercise. We
   give it away because the product does 90% of the analysis.
3. **It's screenshot-able.** The video and the Reveal screen are both
   shareable — every Autopsy is a piece of organic content. Permission
   to repost (anonymized) is asked on send.
4. **It bypasses the "I already have a CRM" objection.** The Autopsy
   isn't a CRM pitch. It's "here are 10 specific dollar amounts you
   can probably still get."
5. **It captures intent without billing.** The trigger to schedule the
   Autopsy is the lead-capture event. The Autopsy is the demo. The
   demo is the close.

**What we deliver in the 5-minute video:**

1. (0:00) "Hey [first], here's the Quote Reclaim Silent Estimate
   Autopsy for [Company]."
2. (0:15) Paste their 10 estimates into Reveal. Show the 3-second
   audit transition.
3. (0:45) "$[total] is sitting silent across these 10. Here are the
   three I'd work first." Click the Top 3 Moves.
4. (1:30) Click into one. Show the Day 1 → Day 30 plan.
5. (3:00) Switch to dashboard view (mock if needed) — show the
   Paid-For-Itself Meter computed off their biggest quote.
6. (4:00) "$49/month is X months covered by one job back. Billing is
   being updated; if you want me to activate the free queue for you
   today I can. Email back and we'll do it."
7. (5:00) "Thanks for sending these over. Reply with the word
   `activate` if you want me to switch on the queue, or `kill it` and
   I'll never email you again."

That last line — explicit one-word kill switch — destroys the
"feels like spam" objection.

## 12. 14-day launch calendar

Daily time budget: **3–5 hours/day**, founder-only.

### Days 1–2 — Prep (no outreach yet)

| Day | Task | Output | Success metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 AM | Buy + set up Google Workspace on `quotereclaim.com`; configure DKIM/SPF/DMARC | Real sending domain | Email passes `mail-tester.com` ≥ 9/10 |
| 1 PM | Pick the metro. Build a 50-row list: Company / Owner first name / Phone / Email / FB page URL | List ready | 50 valid rows |
| 2 AM | Record the generic 60-second product walk-through Loom (keep it private; use for demos) | Demo video URL | < 90 sec, single take, no slides |
| 2 PM | Set up Calendly: "Silent Estimate Autopsy — 15 min" with 3 slots/day | Booking link | Live link |
| 2 PM | Warm up sending domain: send 10 personal emails from `support@quotereclaim.com` to friends/family asking for a reply | Sending reputation seeded | 10 replies recorded |

### Days 3–5 — First outreach wave

Daily template (repeat days 3, 4, 5):

- 25 personalized cold emails to fresh prospects.
- 25 Facebook business-page DMs to a different slice of the list.
- 5 phone calls to prospects who haven't replied to email/DM in 48h
  (skip on day 3 — give email a chance).
- 1 follow-up to every day-3 email non-responder (on day 5).

| Day | Sent | Expected reply | Expected Autopsy bookings |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 3 | 25 email + 25 DM | 2–4 | 0–1 |
| 4 | 25 email + 25 DM + 25 day-3 follow-ups | 4–7 | 1–2 |
| 5 | 25 email + 25 DM + 25 day-3 follow-ups + 5 calls | 5–9 | 1–3 |

End-of-day 5 target: **2+ Autopsies booked, 1+ Autopsy delivered.**

### Days 6–10 — Demos and the second wave

- Continue outreach at **15 emails/day** (down from 25 — protect
  deliverability).
- **Deliver one Autopsy per day** to anyone who replied.
- Phone-call any prospect who watched the Autopsy video (Loom tells
  you) but didn't reply.
- Day 8: review what's working — keep top 2 subject lines, kill the
  rest.
- Day 10: send the day-3 cohort the **postcard follow-up** (5
  postcards at $6 each = $30).

End-of-day-10 target: **3–5 Autopsies delivered, 1+ contractor using
the free product, 1+ contractor verbally OK with $49.**

### Days 11–14 — Iterate, close, decide

- Day 11: write up the script variants that pulled replies. The 2
  winners become the templates.
- Day 12: send the **highest-intent 5 contractors** a "want me to
  activate the free queue today?" email.
- Day 13: schedule a real-time onboarding session for anyone who
  said yes. Live-walk them through the Reveal on their own data.
- Day 14: **kill/continue decision** (see Metrics).

### Pain-validated signal (qualitative)

A contractor says something like:
- "Yeah, I literally have like $40k of these sitting."
- "Half my quotes I just never hear back on."
- "Mrs. Johnson — that one. That bid's been quiet two months."

Three of those = pain validated.

### Pricing-validated signal

A contractor says (unprompted):
- "What is it, like $50 a month?"
- "Yeah, $49 — one job."
- "If it gets me one quote back it pays for itself."

One unprompted line like that = pricing not the bottleneck. (If
they ask "is there a discount" you're fine; if they say "way too much
for what it does" you're not.)

### Kill/continue decision (day 14)

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| ≥ 3 Autopsies delivered AND ≥ 1 pricing-validated commit | **Continue.** Double the outreach for days 15–28. |
| ≥ 3 Autopsies, no pricing commit | **Continue, kill the price test.** Wait on billing; keep the free product growing and prove activation. |
| 0–2 Autopsies | **Kill the script, not the strategy.** Outreach copy is the bottleneck. Rewrite the subject line and one of the email versions, restart with the saved list. |
| < 10 replies total | **Kill the channel mix.** Deliverability is broken or the niche is wrong. Audit DKIM/SPF/DMARC; reconfirm the metro. |

## 14. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold email reputation tanks** | High if domain isn't warmed | Critical | Warm up; cap at 25/day on a new domain; never send the same body twice in a 24h window. |
| **Lands in Promotions tab** | High | Med | Plain text, real founder name, no images, no marketing trackers in v1 emails. |
| **Facebook locks DM-ing pages as spam** | Medium | Med | Send only via personal Facebook to business pages; reference one specific thing from their page; one message per page, ever. |
| **Wrong niche picked** | Medium | High | Day 14 kill/continue decision; the list is reusable across niches. |
| **Contractor trust on PII (sending estimates)** | Medium | Med | Ask only for name + amount, never address/phone; explicitly state "I delete the file after the video." |
| **Billing not live → can't close the call** | Certain | Low | The Autopsy is a no-close call. Capture intent (`activate` keyword), activate by hand, follow up when billing is live. |
| **Looks like debt collection / financial services** | Low (product is honest) | Critical | Never use the words: "recover money for you," "guaranteed," "collect," "debt," "owe." Stick to "the estimate you already sent." |
| **You run out of energy on day 9** | High | High | Daily checklist in a sheet; reward yourself after every Autopsy delivered. |
| **You answer "how much?" too early** | High | High | Always answer "first 3 are free, no card. After that it's $49/month and billing's being updated." Never lead with price. |
| **You write back to "send me info" with a brochure** | High | Med | The Autopsy IS the brochure. "Sure — send me 10 estimates and I'll record it for you." |
| **No replies at all for 5 days** | Possible | Critical | Day 5 is the deliverability-check checkpoint. Run `mail-tester.com` again; pause and fix before sending more. |

## 15. Final recommendation — what to do today

**The first move, in order, today:**

1. **Pick the metro.** Pick one you can drive to. Write it on a
   sticky note. Don't second-guess.

2. **Open Google Maps.** Search `roofing contractor [metro]`. Open
   25 listings. For each, capture:
   - Company name
   - Owner first name (Google "owner [Company]" or check About)
   - Phone
   - Email (from website footer, or Hunter free tier for the
     domain)
   - Facebook business page URL
   - One specific thing: a recent 5-star review name, a recent FB
     post, a service they highlight
   
   Time budget: 90 minutes for 25 prospects.

3. **Send ONE email today** — version 1 from `OUTREACH_SCRIPTS.md`,
   personalized with the specific-thing field. Send from a working
   email address you read every hour. If you don't have
   `support@quotereclaim.com` set up yet, use your personal — get
   one reply, then set up the domain.

4. **Watch the inbox for 24 hours.** Do not blast 50 emails on day
   1. The reply rate on email #1 determines what email #2 looks
   like.

5. **If they reply with curiosity:** Reply with the Autopsy offer.
   Send the Calendly link.

6. **If they reply hostile:** Apologize, ask one question ("would
   it be useful if it cost $0 and took 5 minutes of your time?"),
   never argue.

7. **If no one replies in 24 hours:** Check `mail-tester.com` with
   the subject and body. Fix deliverability before sending email
   #2. Do not blame the message until you've confirmed the inbox.

That is the entire first day. Five steps. No money spent. No tools
bought. No new code shipped.

## Self-check (the user asked me to challenge my own plan)

> *Is this realistic with $100–$200?*

Yes. The plan spends $0 in the first 5 days. The first paid item is
Google Workspace at $7/month, only after day-2 emails come back.
Everything else is founder time.

> *Can a solo founder execute it?*

Yes, but only if the founder is honest about the daily time
commitment. 14 days × 4 hours = 56 hours. That's a part-time job. If
you can't commit that, scale the plan: 7 days × 2 hours, 25
prospects, no fancy infrastructure, just the raw outreach.

> *Does it produce conversations fast?*

Yes — the Autopsy is the conversation. The metric is **Autopsies
delivered**, not Autopsies sold. By day 10 you should have had real
contractor voices.

> *Does it avoid dependency on paid ads?*

Yes. Paid ads are explicitly disallowed in the first $100. The $30
contingency line for "one ad test" only triggers after you have ≥3
paying-ready users to use as social proof.

> *Does it fit the contractor market?*

Yes — Google Maps is where independent contractors live; Facebook
business pages are how they communicate; phone is how they close.
LinkedIn, paid social, and TikTok are eliminated for the right
reasons.

> *Does it generate proof before scale?*

Yes — the day-14 kill/continue decision is the proof gate. You don't
ship version 2 of the marketing without 3+ Autopsies delivered and at
least 1 pricing-validated signal.

> *Does it respect the disabled billing state?*

Yes — the offer never says "buy." It says "free Autopsy, free queue,
billing's being updated, founding rate when it goes live." No fake
checkout. No promise of future pricing changes. No dark patterns.

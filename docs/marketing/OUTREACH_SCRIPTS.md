# Outreach scripts — Quote Reclaim launch

Every script in this file obeys the same five rules:

1. **No fake urgency, no countdown, no scarcity.**
2. **No "guaranteed recovery," no "we recover your money."**
3. **Never positions the product as debt collection or financial services.**
4. **Names the audience and trade in plain English.**
5. **Always offers an explicit exit** (a one-word reply that stops all
   contact). Spam protection is built in.

The variables in square brackets are personalized per prospect:

- `[FIRST]` — owner's first name
- `[COMPANY]` — company name (use exactly how they write it)
- `[METRO]` — city or metro
- `[SPECIFIC]` — one specific thing from their page or a review
- `[YOU]` — your first name only

## Objection replies

### "what is this?"

1. *Short.* "A dashboard for roofers — you paste the last 30
   estimates you sent, it ranks which ones are still worth chasing,
   and writes the follow-up. First 3 free. I'll run yours on a free
   5-min video if you want."
2. *Math-led.* "Tool for the bids you sent that nobody replied to.
   One $5k job back covers 63 months of it. $79/month after the
   first 3 free."
3. *Concrete.* "Quote Reclaim. You give it 10 estimates — name and
   amount — and it shows which are still alive and what to text
   each one. Free 5-min video on your actual numbers if you want."

### "I already have a CRM"

1. "Got it — this isn't a CRM. It looks at the estimates you've
   already sent and tells you which ones are still worth chasing.
   Most CRMs don't do that, they just store the contact. Want me
   to run a quick free Autopsy and you can compare?"
2. "Fair. Quote Reclaim doesn't replace it — it tells you which old
   bids to chase from inside whatever you're using. Want me to
   show you the 5-min Autopsy and you decide?"
3. "Cool — what do you use? — Yeah, [CRM] is solid for tracking
   leads. Quote Reclaim is for the next layer: which OLD estimates
   are still alive. Different problem, no overlap. I'll run a free
   Autopsy on your numbers if you want — 5 min, no sign-up."

### "I don't pay for software"

1. "Totally fair. First 3 quotes are free, no card needed. The
   Autopsy I'd do for you is free too. You can use the free part
   forever — pricing kicks in beyond 3 active quote sequences."
2. "Heard that. Worth the free Autopsy at least? It's 5 minutes,
   you send 10 estimates, I send back a video. If $79 doesn't feel
   worth it after, you keep the free queue forever."
3. "Most contractors I talk to say that, then one $5k job comes
   back and the math flips. First 3 are free — try them, don't pay
   anything, see what you think."

### "send me info"

1. "The fastest 'info' I can send is the free Autopsy. Send 10
   estimates — just name and amount — to [EMAIL] and I'll record
   a 5-min video with your real ranked list. No deck."
2. "Sure. The 90-second version: roofers send estimates, half go
   quiet, Quote Reclaim ranks which are still worth chasing and
   writes the follow-up. The other 'info' is just a 5-min video
   on your own numbers if you want — free."
3. "Best 'info' is your own numbers. Send 10 estimates, I send
   back the Autopsy video the same day. Otherwise quotereclaim.com
   is the one-pager."

### "how much?"

1. "$79/month. First 3 quotes are free, no card needed. Billing's
   being updated right now — when it relaunches, the people who
   used the Autopsy get founding pricing locked in."
2. "$79/month after the free 3. One job back covers a few years of
   it."
3. "$79/month. Honest math: $5k job ÷ $79 = 63 months. You're not
   really paying for it; you're paying for one job not slipping
   through."

### "does it guarantee jobs?"

1. "No — and I don't trust anyone who says they do. It shows you
   which old estimates are still worth a follow-up and writes the
   message. Whether the job comes back depends on the homeowner.
   Math anchor: if even one $5k job comes back, the product covers
   itself for 63 months."
2. "No guarantee. It's a tool — points at the bids most likely
   still alive and gives you the words to send. The win or loss is
   between you and the homeowner."
3. "No. That kind of language would get me in trouble and
   probably lose your trust. What I can promise: it'll show you
   3–10 old estimates you'd otherwise forget. The follow-up is on
   you."

## Compliance notes

- Every cold-outreach message above has an explicit one-word **STOP**
  / **stop** / **kill it** exit. Always honor it within the hour.
- Never send "you owe," "you lost," "we recover your money," or any
  variation. Always speak about *the estimate you already wrote up*.
- Never claim "guaranteed," "we'll recover," or "automated recovery."
- The product's automated email follow-up is the only real automation
  claim — never imply automated phone calls, SMS, or in-person
  follow-up.
- Keep cold email volume ≤ 25/day on a fresh domain. After day 14, if
  reputation is clean, scale to 50/day max.
